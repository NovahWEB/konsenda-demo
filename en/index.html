Isla Popa · Caribbean archipelago

Bocas del Toro

An eco-lodge and sanctuary on Isla Popa, where forest, mangrove and reef meet. Here the strawberry frog changes color island to island, and the sea keeps a science of its own.

What you will see See all species
  • Amphibian

    Red-eyed tree frog

    Agalychnis callidryas

    The most iconic frog of the American tropics. It shows up at night on leaves above the forest pools.
  • Amphibian

    Dink frog

    Diasporus sp.

    Tiny and high-pitched, it shows up at night among the leaf litter of the Bocas forest. You hear it long before you see it.
  • Mammal

    Sloth

    Bradypus sp.

    A calm dweller of the Bocas canopy. With a sharp eye and a guide, it turns up hanging from the trail branches.

Km 11 Llano–Cartí road · cloud mountain

San Blas Mountain

The cloud mountain, not the islands: continuous forest and home of the Limosa harlequin toad. At night the forest comes alive and we head out frog-hunting with the biologists.

Dwellers of the mist See all species
  • Amphibian · emblem

    Limosa harlequin toad

    Atelopus limosus

    Critically endangered. The San Blas stream is one of the last places where it is counted, season after season.
  • Amphibian

    Glass frog

    Hyalinobatrachium sp.

    Its transparent belly reveals the heart and organs. It appears on leaves over the streams during night searches.
  • Bird

    Trogon

    Trogon sp.

    One of the jewels of highland birding. Part of the 400+ bird species recorded across our lodges.
  • Bird

    White-necked jacobin

    Florisuga mellivora

    Visits the hummingbird feeders right outside the cabins. A photographer favorite for its blue hood.
