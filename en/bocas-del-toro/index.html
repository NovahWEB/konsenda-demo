Isla Popa · Caribbean archipelago

Bocas del Toro

An eco-lodge and wildlife sanctuary on Isla Popa, where the rainforest meets mangrove and reef. Poison frogs, tropical birds, kayaking and snorkeling, all in one place.

The lodge was built from local materials by local hands, and serves as a base for wildlife observation and conservation projects. Every stay includes one nature experience per day —from birding at dawn to the forest after dark— and part of every reservation funds scientific and community work across the archipelago.

Getting there

Access by sea. Boat transfer from Almirante (about 45 minutes).

Experiences at this lodge

  • Daytime herping

    Poison frog tour

    The strawberry frog and its colors shifting from island to island. A walk through the sanctuary to understand why Bocas is an open-air evolution lab.

    From USD $60 Half day
  • Night herping

    The forest after dark

    A night walk along the sanctuary trails: frogs, insects, eyes that shine. The Bocas forest becomes another place once the sun goes down.

    From USD $60 Night · 1.5 hours
  • Birding

    Lowland birding

    A birding dawn on the Caribbean slope, part of the 400+ species recorded. Parrots, frigatebirds and mangrove birds from the dock and the trails.

    From USD $50 Dawn · 3 hours
